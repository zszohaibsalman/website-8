Legal Framework in Pakistan

Contract law in Pakistan is governed by various laws and regulations:

  • Contract Act, 1872: The primary legislation governing contracts in Pakistan
  • Specific Relief Act, 1877: Provides remedies for specific performance and injunctions
  • Sale of Goods Act, 1930: Governs contracts for the sale of goods
  • Arbitration Act, 1940: Governs arbitration proceedings
  • Code of Civil Procedure, 1908: Governs the procedure for civil litigation, including contract disputes
  • Qanoon-e-Shahadat Order, 1984: Governs the law of evidence in contract disputes
  • Islamic Law: Islamic principles governing contracts and commercial transactions

Our team has in-depth knowledge of these laws and regulations, ensuring that your contracts are legally sound and enforceable under Pakistani law.
