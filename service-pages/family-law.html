Divorce & Khula

We handle all types of divorce proceedings under Pakistani law, including:

  • Talaq: Divorce initiated by the husband under Islamic law
  • Khula: Divorce initiated by the wife with return of dower (mehr)
  • Mutual Divorce: Divorce by mutual consent under the Dissolution of Muslim Marriages Act, 1939
  • Judicial Divorce: Divorce through family courts on valid grounds

Our experienced lawyers guide you through the entire process, ensuring your rights are protected and the proceedings are handled efficiently.

Child Custody

Under Pakistani law, child custody matters are governed by the Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 and Islamic principles. We provide expert legal representation in:

  • Custody Disputes: Representation in family courts for custody matters
  • Visitation Rights: Establishing fair visitation schedules
  • Guardianship Orders: Legal proceedings for guardianship of minors
  • International Custody: Cases involving cross-border custody issues

The welfare of the child is the paramount consideration in Pakistani courts. We work diligently to ensure the best interests of your children are protected.

Maintenance & Alimony

We handle maintenance claims under the Family Courts Act, 1964 and Islamic law, including:

  • Wife Maintenance: Claims for maintenance during marriage and after divorce
  • Child Support: Claims for financial support of children
  • Enforcement: Legal proceedings to enforce maintenance orders
  • Modification: Requests to modify existing maintenance orders

Our lawyers ensure that fair and adequate maintenance is awarded based on the financial circumstances of both parties.

Marriage & Nikah

We provide comprehensive legal services related to marriage under Pakistani law:

  • Nikah Registration: Proper registration of Islamic marriages
  • Civil Marriages: Registration of civil marriages under the Christian Marriage Act and other laws
  • Court Marriages: Legal assistance for court marriages
  • Marriage Contracts: Drafting and reviewing marriage contracts (Nikahnama)
  • Polygamy Issues: Legal advice on polygamous marriages under Pakistani law

Our team ensures that all marriages are properly registered and legally valid, protecting your rights under Pakistani law.

Guardianship

Under the Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, we handle all matters related to guardianship of minors:

  • Guardianship Applications: Legal proceedings to appoint guardians
  • Guardianship Disputes: Resolution of disputes between potential guardians
  • Property Guardianship: Management of minor's property
  • International Guardianship: Cases involving guardianship across borders

The welfare of the minor is the primary consideration in guardianship matters. We ensure that the best interests of the child are protected.

Adoption

While Islamic law does not recognize adoption in the Western sense, Pakistani law provides for alternative arrangements:

  • Foster Care: Legal arrangements for foster care under Pakistani law
  • Guardianship: Legal guardianship arrangements for orphaned or abandoned children
  • International Adoption: Assistance with international adoption processes
  • Inheritance Rights: Legal advice on inheritance rights for adopted children

Our team provides expert guidance on the legal options available for those seeking to provide care for children in need.

Domestic Violence

We provide legal protection and representation for victims of domestic violence under the Domestic Violence (Prevention and Protection) Act, 2012:

  • Protection Orders: Obtaining protection orders from courts
  • Residence Orders: Securing safe housing arrangements
  • Monetary Relief: Claims for financial compensation
  • Custody Orders: Securing custody of children in domestic violence cases
  • Criminal Proceedings: Assistance with criminal complaints against abusers

Our team provides compassionate and confidential legal support to victims of domestic violence, helping them secure their safety and legal rights.

Inheritance & Succession

We handle inheritance matters under Pakistani law, including Islamic inheritance rules and statutory provisions:

  • Succession Certificates: Legal proceedings to obtain succession certificates
  • Will Drafting: Preparation of legally valid wills under Pakistani law
  • Probate: Legal proceedings for probate of wills
  • Inheritance Disputes: Resolution of disputes among heirs
  • Islamic Inheritance: Calculation and distribution of assets according to Islamic law

Our team ensures that inheritance matters are handled efficiently and in accordance with the law, protecting the rights of all parties involved.

Legal Framework in Pakistan

Family law in Pakistan is governed by a combination of Islamic principles and statutory laws:

  • Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961: Governs marriage, divorce, maintenance, and polygamy
  • Dissolution of Muslim Marriages Act, 1939: Provides grounds for dissolution of Muslim marriages
  • Guardians and Wards Act, 1890: Governs guardianship and custody matters
  • Family Courts Act, 1964: Establishes family courts for resolution of family disputes
  • Domestic Violence (Prevention and Protection) Act, 2012: Provides protection against domestic violence

Our team has in-depth knowledge of these laws and their application in Pakistani courts, ensuring that your case is handled with expertise and professionalism.
