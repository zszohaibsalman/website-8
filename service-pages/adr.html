Mediation

Mediation is a voluntary, confidential process where a neutral third party (the mediator) facilitates negotiations between disputing parties to help them reach a mutually acceptable settlement:

  • Family Mediation: Mediation for family disputes including divorce, child custody, and inheritance matters
  • Commercial Mediation: Mediation for business and commercial disputes
  • Employment Mediation: Mediation for workplace disputes and employment conflicts
  • Property Mediation: Mediation for property disputes and real estate conflicts
  • Community Mediation: Mediation for community disputes and neighborhood conflicts
  • Court-Annexed Mediation: Mediation conducted under court supervision
  • Online Mediation: Virtual mediation conducted through online platforms

Our experienced mediators facilitate constructive dialogue, helping parties explore options and reach mutually beneficial agreements that preserve relationships and avoid the costs of litigation.

Arbitration

Arbitration is a private dispute resolution process where parties agree to submit their disputes to one or more neutral arbitrators who make a binding decision:

  • Domestic Arbitration: Arbitration conducted within Pakistan under the Arbitration Act, 1940
  • International Arbitration: Arbitration for cross-border disputes under international arbitration rules
  • Commercial Arbitration: Arbitration for business and commercial disputes
  • Construction Arbitration: Arbitration for construction and infrastructure disputes
  • Investment Arbitration: Arbitration for investment disputes and investor-state conflicts
  • Award Enforcement: Enforcement of arbitral awards under the New York Convention
  • Institutional Arbitration: Arbitration conducted under the rules of arbitration institutions

Our experienced arbitrators provide fair, efficient, and binding resolution of disputes, ensuring that your case is decided by experts with specialized knowledge in the relevant field.

Negotiation

Negotiation is a direct dialogue between parties to resolve disputes and reach mutually acceptable agreements:

  • Contract Negotiation: Negotiation of contract terms and conditions
  • Settlement Negotiation: Negotiation of settlement agreements in litigation
  • Business Negotiation: Negotiation of business deals and transactions
  • Employment Negotiation: Negotiation of employment terms and severance packages
  • Pre-Litigation Negotiation: Negotiation to resolve disputes before filing lawsuits
  • International Negotiation: Negotiation of cross-border disputes and transactions
  • Assisted Negotiation: Negotiation with the assistance of legal counsel

Our skilled negotiators help you achieve favorable outcomes through strategic negotiation, preserving relationships and avoiding the costs of litigation.

Conciliation

Conciliation is a process where a neutral third party (the conciliator) assists disputing parties in reaching a mutually acceptable settlement:

  • Labor Conciliation: Conciliation for labor disputes and industrial conflicts
  • Commercial Conciliation: Conciliation for business and commercial disputes
  • Family Conciliation: Conciliation for family disputes and domestic conflicts
  • Community Conciliation: Conciliation for community disputes and neighborhood conflicts
  • Government Conciliation: Conciliation involving government agencies and public bodies
  • International Conciliation: Conciliation for cross-border disputes

Our experienced conciliators facilitate constructive dialogue and propose solutions to help parties reach amicable settlements that preserve relationships and avoid litigation.

Neutral Evaluation

Neutral evaluation is a process where a neutral expert evaluates the strengths and weaknesses of each party's case and provides a non-binding assessment:

  • Legal Evaluation: Assessment of legal merits and potential outcomes
  • Technical Evaluation: Assessment of technical issues in complex disputes
  • Financial Evaluation: Assessment of financial claims and damages
  • Early Case Assessment: Early evaluation of case strengths and weaknesses
  • Settlement Facilitation: Use of evaluation to facilitate settlement negotiations
  • Expert Determination: Binding determination by subject matter experts

Our neutral evaluators provide objective assessments of your case, helping you make informed decisions about settlement and litigation strategy.

Mini-Trials

Mini-trials are structured settlement processes where parties present abbreviated versions of their cases to a neutral advisor or panel:

  • Case Presentation: Presentation of key evidence and arguments by each party
  • Neutral Advisor: Evaluation and feedback from a neutral advisor
  • Executive Involvement: Participation of senior executives with settlement authority
  • Settlement Negotiation: Facilitated settlement negotiations following case presentation
  • Confidentiality: Confidential proceedings that do not prejudice litigation
  • Complex Disputes: Effective for complex commercial and technical disputes

Our mini-trial process provides a realistic assessment of case strengths and weaknesses, facilitating informed settlement decisions and avoiding the costs of full litigation.

Ombudsman Services

We provide assistance with ombudsman proceedings and alternative dispute resolution through government ombudsman offices:

  • Federal Ombudsman: Complaints and representation before the Federal Ombudsman (Wafaqi Mohtasib)
  • Provincial Ombudsmen: Complaints and representation before provincial ombudsmen
  • Banking Ombudsman: Complaints against banks and financial institutions
  • Insurance Ombudsman: Complaints against insurance companies
  • Tax Ombudsman: Complaints against tax authorities
  • SECP Ombudsman: Complaints against securities and exchange matters
  • Complaint Preparation: Preparation and filing of ombudsman complaints

Our team assists clients in navigating ombudsman proceedings, providing an efficient and cost-effective alternative to litigation for resolving disputes with government agencies and regulated entities.

ADR Clauses

We draft and review alternative dispute resolution clauses for inclusion in contracts and agreements:

  • Mediation Clauses: Clauses requiring mediation before litigation or arbitration
  • Arbitration Clauses: Clauses requiring arbitration of disputes
  • Multi-Tiered Clauses: Clauses requiring multiple ADR processes before litigation
  • Institutional Clauses: Clauses specifying ADR institutions and rules
  • Governing Law: Clauses specifying governing law and jurisdiction for ADR
  • Enforcement Clauses: Clauses addressing enforcement of ADR outcomes

Our team ensures that your contracts include effective ADR clauses that provide clear, enforceable mechanisms for resolving disputes efficiently and cost-effectively.

Legal Framework in Pakistan

Alternative dispute resolution in Pakistan is governed by various laws and regulations:

  • Arbitration Act, 1940: Governs domestic arbitration proceedings
  • Recognition and Enforcement (Arbitration Agreements and Foreign Arbitral Awards) Act, 2011: Governs enforcement of foreign arbitral awards
  • Code of Civil Procedure, 1908: Provides for court-annexed mediation and ADR
  • Family Courts Act, 1964: Provides for mediation in family disputes
  • Industrial Relations Act, 2012: Provides for conciliation in labor disputes
  • Wafaqi Mohtasib (Ombudsman) Order, 1983: Establishes the Federal Ombudsman institution
  • Contract Act, 1872: Governs the formation and enforcement of ADR agreements
  • New York Convention: Pakistan is a signatory to the New York Convention on enforcement of arbitral awards

Our team has in-depth knowledge of these laws and regulations, ensuring that your ADR processes are conducted efficiently and in compliance with Pakistani law.
