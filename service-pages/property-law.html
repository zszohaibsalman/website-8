Property Transfers

We handle all aspects of property transfers under Pakistani law, including:

  • Sale Deeds: Preparation and registration of sale deeds for property transfers
  • Gift Deeds: Preparation and registration of gift deeds for property transfers
  • Exchange Deeds: Preparation and registration of exchange deeds for property swaps
  • Partition Deeds: Preparation and registration of partition deeds for division of jointly owned property
  • Mutation: Mutation of property records with revenue authorities
  • Stamp Duty: Payment of stamp duty and registration fees
  • Registration: Registration of property documents with the relevant sub-registrar office

Our team ensures that property transfers are legally valid, properly documented, and registered with the appropriate authorities.

Title Verification

Legal Framework in Pakistan

Property law in Pakistan is governed by various laws and regulations:

  • Transfer of Property Act, 1882: Governs the transfer of property rights
  • Registration Act, 1908: Governs the registration of property documents
  • Stamp Act, 1899: Governs the payment of stamp duty on property documents
  • Land Revenue Act, 1967: Governs land revenue administration and records
  • Specific Relief Act, 1877: Provides remedies for specific performance and injunctions
  • Rent Restriction Ordinance, 1959: Governs rent control and landlord-tenant relationships
  • Islamic Law: Islamic principles governing property rights and inheritance
  • Local Laws: Provincial and local laws governing property matters

Our team has in-depth knowledge of these laws and regulations, ensuring that your property matters are handled efficiently and effectively under Pakistani law.
