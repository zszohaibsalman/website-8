Bail Applications

We handle all types of bail applications under Pakistani law, including:

  • Pre-Arrest Bail: Applications under Section 498 of the Code of Criminal Procedure to prevent arrest
  • Post-Arrest Bail: Applications for bail after arrest under Section 497 of the Code of Criminal Procedure
  • Anticipatory Bail: Applications for bail in anticipation of arrest
  • Regular Bail: Applications for bail after arrest in non-bailable offenses
  • Bail in High Court: Bail applications filed in the High Court
  • Bail in Supreme Court: Bail applications filed in the Supreme Court of Pakistan

Our experienced criminal defense lawyers have a strong track record of securing bail for our clients, ensuring their freedom while their cases proceed through the legal system.

Legal Framework in Pakistan

Criminal law in Pakistan is governed by various laws and procedural rules:

  • Pakistan Penal Code, 1860: The primary criminal code defining offenses and punishments
  • Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898: Governs the procedure for criminal cases
  • Qanoon-e-Shahadat Order, 1984: Governs the law of evidence in criminal cases
  • Anti-Terrorism Act, 1997: Governs terrorism-related offenses and procedures
  • Control of Narcotic Substances Act, 1997: Governs narcotics-related offenses
  • Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act, 2016: Governs cyber crimes
  • Juvenile Justice System Ordinance, 2000: Governs the treatment of juvenile offenders

Our team has in-depth knowledge of these laws and procedural rules, ensuring that your case is handled efficiently and effectively in the Pakistani criminal justice system.
